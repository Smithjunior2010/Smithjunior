READ MY STORY

Prud'homme Smith Junior

My name is Prud'homme Smith Junior, Raised in Ouanaminthe(Haiti) by a family with limited economic means, my educational journey began at Rose Marie Kindergarten, followed by FIC, and my secondary education at Institution Univers.

From a young age, I harbored a fascination for computers. This passion led me to enter BYU (Brigham Young University) Online after high school, where I immersed myself in a year of computer programming. During this time, I seized the opportunity to work with an impactful organization in Haiti, the Haitian Education Project (HEP). In 2021, I was an English student, and now, I proudly serve as an English teacher at the Ouanaminthe center. I also had the opportunity to work for Institution la Salesienne and Jean Paul II as an English teacher and as an assistant for Math, Chemistry, and Physics for secondary students. My proficiency in English allowed me to serve my community as a translator for Mission Life organization, where I was eager to meet many good American friends.
My passion lies in serving those in my vicinity, advocating for fellow young individuals, actively participating in issue resolution, engaging in discussions, and collaborating effectively within groups.

Equipped with newfound skills in computer programming, I am eager to pursue a degree in Computer Science, a field that I believe plays a crucial role in the development and progress of Haiti.
